Brain Computer Interfaces (BCI)

This Fund's Mission

The brain is the single most complicated object in the universe. Today, our understanding of the human brain is in its infancy, and we need better implants to prevent nerve degradation and scarring, better decodings of brain activity, and better ways to record neurons.

A brain-computer interface will be as revolutionary as mobile computing, and give everyone a high-bandwidth connection to the Internet and assistants like Siri or Google.

Featured Labs

LewPea Lab

The LewPea Lab is understanding how the brain deals with memory and learning. With real-time brain data, the lab looks at moment-to-moment patterns in the brain and identify what a person is seeing, retrieving from memory, holding in mind, and even forgetting.

At a glance

  • 9 PhD students and postdoctoral researchers
  • Dr. Lewis-Peacock was recently awarded the Services for Students with Disabilities award, and his work has implications for memories of fear, trauma, and safety.

    Lu Research Group

    The Lu Research Group is manufacturing the next generation of flexible and stretchable technology to interface with the human body.

    At a glance

  • 12 PhD students and postdoctoral researchers (4 in engineering mechanics, 5 in electrical engineering, 2 in materials science, and 1 in mechanical engineering).
  • Dr. Lu has 60 peer reviewed articles with over 5600 citations by other researchers. She has 1 issued patent and 5 currently pending.
