Brain Computer Interfaces (BCI)

This Fund's Mission

The brain is the single most complicated object in the universe. Today, our understanding of the human brain is in its infancy, and we need better implants to prevent nerve degradation and scarring, better decodings of brain activity, and better ways to record neurons.

A brain-computer interface will be as revolutionary as mobile computing, and give everyone a high-bandwidth connection to the Internet and assistants like Siri or Google.

Featured Labs

LewPea Lab

The LewPea Lab is understanding how the brain deals with memory and learning. With real-time brain data, the lab looks at moment-to-moment patterns in the brain to identify what a person is seeing, retrieving from memory, holding in mind, and even forgetting.

At a glance

  • 9 PhD / postdoctoral researchers
  • Dr. Lewis-Peacock was recently awarded the Services for Students with Disabilities award, on work with memories of fear, trauma, and safety.
    •

    Show More

    Why we chose this lab

    When the human brain makes any decisions, it leans on vast archives of stored memory. Understanding the process of retrieval could one day lead to implants that make it easy to remember anything, or allow therapy patients to selectively forget painful or traumatic memories. We chose this lab because of Professor Lewis-Peacock's innovative approach of correlating real-time brain data with memory retrieval.

    Projects From this Lab

    1. Understanding what a person is seeing, remembering, or forgetting in real-time using fMRIs

    2. Creating a real-time neurofeedback platform to help stroke patients recover

    3. Training people to improve their memory abilities or to strategically impair them

    Lu Research Group

    The Lu Research Group is manufacturing the next generation of flexible and stretchable devices to interface with the human body.

    At a glance

  • 12 PhD / postdoctoral researchers (4 in engineering mechanics, 5 in electrical engineering, 2 in materials science, and 1 in mechanical engineering).
  • Dr. Lu has 60 peer reviewed articles with over 5600 citations by other researchers. She has 1 issued patent and 5 currently pending.
    •

    Show More

    Why we chose this lab

    A significant problem with the current generation of brain-machine interfaces is smooth integration into the human body. Bioelectronics will have to be significantly more flexible, cheap, and imperceptible to be usable at scale to record from neurons. The Lu Research Group is currently making incredible strides in these active areas of research.

    Projects From this Lab

    1. Easy and simple manufacturing of electronic-tattoos (e-tattoos)

    2. Wireless and disposable tattoos to record breathing, temperature, hydration, and oxygen saturation

    3. Designing imperceptible and ultra-conformable surfaces using graphene

    4. Studying the adhesion between soft materials and 2D materials
